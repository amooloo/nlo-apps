Collection Guidelines

  1. Run AR Aging Reports regularly.
  2. Follow up with phone calls, text and email.
  3. Focus on 1–30 days past due — you have the highest chance of collection here.
  4. Make an Alternative Arrangement (AA) as soon as trouble is sensed.
  5. Come up with a realistic plan for the RP.
  6. If AA is broken, change appointment to Deb/Finish and send certified and regular postal letters.
  7. If a situation, such as job loss, has caused financial difficulties that make it challenging to continue orthodontic treatment, it may be appropriate to discuss the possibility of discontinuing (D/C) treatment with Dr. Akhavan.

Authorized Individuals for Record Disclosure

Names of individuals the patient authorizes NLO to share medical record info, appointment details, and financial information with (written on printed form).

FC Reminders

  1. Give patient/RP a copy of the current Notice of Privacy Practices.
  2. Explain each photo consent option — patient can say Yes or No to each independently.
  3. Signing is voluntary — refusal does not affect treatment.
  4. Patient may revoke authorization at any time in writing.
  5. Authorization expires when the patient is no longer a patient of NLO, unless revoked sooner.
  6. If patient refuses to sign NPP acknowledgement, document the reason in the Office Use section.

HIPAA Compliance Notes

  1. 45 CFR 164.508 — Authorization must include: description of info, who discloses, who receives, purpose, expiration, signature/date, right to revoke, treatment not conditioned on signing, and redisclosure warning.
  2. Fla. Stat. 456.057 — Florida requires written authorization for any disclosure. Third parties cannot re-disclose without patient’s express written consent.
  3. 2026 NPP Update — As of Feb 16, 2026, NPP must address substance use disorder (SUD) record protections per 42 CFR Part 2 alignment.
  4. Photos are PHI even outside the medical record if they can identify a patient.
  5. Maintain a log of all disclosures per Florida law.
